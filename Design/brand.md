# BooBoo Brand Guide

## Name Origin

BooBoo combines "boo" (the sound a ghost makes) with a doubled playful syllable. The name evokes a friendly ghost that watches over macOS privacy — visible enough to remind you it is there, never scary. The "Boo" is a direct ghost reference; the reduplicated "BooBoo" softens it into something approachable.

## Purpose

BooBoo is a macOS privacy watchdog. It monitors CIS benchmark compliance, alerts on configuration drift, and provides actionable remediation guidance. It is not a nag: it speaks when it matters and stays quiet the rest of the time.

## Tone

Friendly-authoritative. BooBoo knows security but does not scold. Notifications are informative, never alarmist. The voice assumes the user wants to be secure but may not know how.

- Good: "Your firewall is off. Turn it on to block unwanted network traffic."
- Avoid: "WARNING: Firewall disabled! Immediate action required!"

## Color Palette

- Primary: Purple (`#6B3FA0`) — the ghost color, friendly but distinctive
- Secondary: Dark Gray (`#2C2C2E`) — system bar and text elements
- Accent: Cyan (`#64D2FF`) — highlights and actionable items
- Background: White (`#FFFFFF`) and Light Gray (`#F2F2F7`)
- Success: Green (`#30D158`)
- Warning: Orange (`#FF9F0A`)
- Error: Red (`#FF453A`)

## Typography

- Primary: SF Pro Rounded (system font on macOS)
- Usage: Titles and headings use SF Pro Rounded Semibold. Body text uses SF Pro Text Regular.
- Fallback: -apple-system, system-ui, sans-serif

## Mascot Direction

A simple ghost silhouette in the BooBoo purple. Minimal — a rounded ghost shape with two small eyes. No cartoon details. The ghost appears in the menu bar icon and in notification headers.
